Munno AI Ugandan Super Agent for organized work and business communication

Privacy Policy

Munno AI helps users manage WhatsApp reminders, Focus Inbox, money alerts, documents, and AI assistant tasks while keeping sensitive data under user control.

Effective date 15 June 2026
Last updated 15 June 2026

Who We Are

Munno AI, also called Munno Super Agent AI, is operated by the Munno AI project team in Uganda. This policy explains what information Munno collects, how it is used, and the choices users have.

Privacy contact: privacy@munno.ai

Private By Default

Munno is built to be private by default. We try to keep sensitive information on your phone where possible, ask before linking accounts, and avoid storing raw private messages unless a feature truly needs it.

What Munno Does

  • WhatsApp companion setup so your assistant can help from your linked WhatsApp number.
  • Reminders created in WhatsApp or in the companion app.
  • Focus Inbox on Android to help organize phone notifications locally.
  • Money alert understanding for MoMo, Airtel Money, and similar payment messages when enabled.
  • Document help, such as CVs, cover letters, and summaries.
  • AI responses in English and supported Ugandan language workflows.
  • Optional email linking, such as Gmail or Outlook, when enabled and approved by the user.

Some features may still be beta, limited, or disabled until they are ready for public launch.

Information We Collect

Munno may collect or process:

  • Phone number, allowed owner number, and WhatsApp session mapping.
  • Setup preferences such as business, work, job-seeking, school, or personal use.
  • Priority keywords, work or business category, language preferences, and app settings.
  • Messages sent directly to Munno, such as reminder requests or assistant commands.
  • Reminder text, due date, time zone, status, and local alarm scheduling data.
  • Notification content on Android only after notification access is granted.
  • Documents, images, PDFs, or voice notes you choose to send or upload.
  • Email metadata or selected message content if email linking is enabled and you approve it.

WhatsApp

When you link WhatsApp, Munno may process the linked session identifier, approved owner number, and messages you send to Munno. Munno is designed to reply only to approved owner or allowed numbers. Messages from other people are ignored unless broader access is explicitly enabled later.

Munno is not an official WhatsApp or Meta product. You can disconnect Munno in the app or remove the linked device from WhatsApp under Linked devices.

Notifications And Focus Inbox

On Android, if you grant notification access, Munno may read notifications shown on your phone so it can help you notice important items like customers, work messages, missed calls, reminders, and money alerts.

  • Raw notification bodies should stay on your device for the launch MVP.
  • Munno should not upload your full notification history by default.
  • Notification access is optional and can be turned off in Android settings.
  • You can clear local notification history in the app where supported.

iOS does not allow normal App Store apps to read arbitrary third-party notifications or the full SMS inbox. Munno will not claim those abilities unless Apple provides an official approved API or entitlement for that use.

Money And Payment Signals

If you enable money-related features, Munno may process payment notification text or messages you provide in order to detect amounts, sender or receiver labels, transaction direction, dates, and summaries.

Munno does not directly access your MTN MoMo, Airtel Money, bank, or telecom account unless a future integration clearly asks for consent through an official provider flow. Do not share PINs, passwords, OTP codes, card numbers, or secret account credentials with Munno.

Email And Business Channels

If email linking is enabled, Munno will ask for consent through the provider, such as Google or Microsoft. Munno should request the minimum scopes needed for the feature. Depending on what you approve, Munno may process email metadata, subject lines, selected message snippets, tasks, deadlines, attachments, or summaries.

Munno should not store full mailbox history by default. You can disconnect linked email accounts where supported.

How We Use Information

  • Provide the assistant, reminders, Focus Inbox, money, and document features.
  • Pair, authorize, and disconnect your WhatsApp assistant.
  • Send replies and reminder confirmations to approved numbers.
  • Prioritize important notifications and reduce noise.
  • Create summaries, tasks, and suggested follow-ups.
  • Improve reliability, security, abuse prevention, and troubleshooting.
  • Comply with legal, store, safety, and platform requirements.

We do not sell your personal information.

AI Processing

Munno may use AI model providers to generate responses, summaries, classifications, and documents. Depending on configuration, providers may include OpenAI, Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, NVIDIA-hosted models, or other configured model providers.

We try to send only the information needed to answer your request. Sensitive data such as raw WhatsApp chats, email bodies, notification bodies, MoMo details, and private documents should not be sent for broad analysis unless you request the feature or give consent.

What Stays On Your Device

The launch MVP is designed so these items stay on your device unless you choose a cloud feature:

  • Focus Inbox notification contents.
  • Local reminder cache.
  • Setup preferences and priority keywords.
  • Local alarm scheduling data.
  • Some app settings and linked-device configuration.

What May Be Stored On Our Servers

  • Phone numbers and allowed owner numbers.
  • WhatsApp session mapping and credentials needed to keep the assistant linked.
  • Reminder records and delivery status.
  • Assistant request and response data needed to complete a task.
  • Generated document output or temporary file references.
  • Account identifiers if sign-in is enabled.
  • Basic logs, errors, and security events.

Production should keep raw message logging disabled unless debugging is explicitly enabled. Debug logs must not expose private user messages in public demos, screenshots, or support tickets.

Sharing Information

We may share information with service providers used to operate Munno, including:

  • Cloud hosting and database providers.
  • AI model providers used to answer your request.
  • Authentication providers such as Firebase, Google, Apple, or Microsoft when enabled.
  • Email providers such as Google or Microsoft when you link those accounts.
  • WhatsApp-related infrastructure needed to operate the companion session.
  • Analytics, crash, or monitoring providers if added later and disclosed in the app.
  • Authorities if required by law.

We do not sell personal data to advertisers or data brokers.

Permissions

  • Notification access on Android for Focus Inbox.
  • Notification permission to show reminder alerts.
  • Microphone permission if voice features are enabled.
  • File, photo, or document access if you choose to upload files.
  • Internet access to connect to Munno services and AI providers.

Permissions are requested for product features. You can deny permissions, but some features may not work.

Retention

  • Local Focus Inbox data: kept on your device until you clear it or uninstall the app.
  • Reminders: kept until deleted, completed, expired, or account data is removed.
  • WhatsApp session credentials: kept while your assistant is linked and removed when disconnected where supported.
  • Email tokens: kept while the email account is linked and revoked or deleted when disconnected where supported.
  • Debug logs: kept for a limited period and should not contain raw private content in production.

Your Choices

  • Disconnect WhatsApp in Munno settings or from WhatsApp Linked devices.
  • Change the owner or allowed phone number.
  • Disable Android notification access in system settings.
  • Clear local Focus Inbox data where supported.
  • Delete reminders.
  • Disconnect linked email accounts where supported.
  • Uninstall the app to remove app-local data from the device.
  • Contact us to request access, correction, export, or deletion of data we control.

Account Deletion

If account sign-in is enabled, Munno should provide an in-app account deletion flow or a clear support request path. Account deletion should remove or deactivate account records, reminders, authorized phones, linked account tokens, and WhatsApp session credentials where technically possible.

Until the in-app deletion flow is available, contact privacy@munno.ai and include the phone number or account email linked to Munno.

Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect information, including permission-based access, private app storage, HTTPS in production, limited logging, and disconnect controls. No system is perfectly secure, especially when linked communications accounts are involved.

  • Only link WhatsApp if you started the setup yourself.
  • Review WhatsApp's linked-device warning carefully.
  • Keep your phone locked.
  • Never share OTPs, PINs, passwords, or mobile money secret codes with Munno.
  • Disconnect Munno immediately if you no longer trust a device or session.

Children

Munno is intended for general productivity and business or personal organization. It is not designed for children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information without appropriate consent, contact us and we will take reasonable steps to delete it.

International Processing

Munno may use service providers outside Uganda. By using Munno, you understand that information may be processed in countries where our providers operate, subject to this policy and applicable safeguards.

Third-Party Services

Munno may interact with WhatsApp, Google, Microsoft, Apple, Firebase, AI providers, and cloud services. Those services have their own terms and privacy policies. Munno is not responsible for third-party privacy practices.

Changes And Contact

We may update this policy as Munno changes. If changes are important, we will update the date above and, where appropriate, notify users in the app or through another reasonable method.
